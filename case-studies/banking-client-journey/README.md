# Banking Client Journey Improvement – Reducing Friction in Service Delivery

## Problem

In regulated banking environments, client onboarding and service processes often involve multiple handoffs across business units, resulting in delays, fragmented communication, and inconsistent client experience.

---

## Context

This case study is based on experience in banking transformation environments where client-facing processes depend on coordination between business teams, operations, and technology functions.

---

## Key Insight

Most friction in client journeys is caused not by lack of systems, but by:

- fragmented ownership across teams
- unclear process handoffs
- manual intervention in otherwise standard workflows

---

## Objective

To simplify and improve a banking client journey by reducing operational friction and improving coordination between stakeholders.

---

## Approach

### 1. Journey Mapping

Identified key stages of the client journey from request initiation to completion.

### 2. Friction Analysis

Highlighted:

- duplicated approvals
- manual data handoffs
- unclear ownership between teams

### 3. Improvement Design

Proposed:

- clearer ownership per stage
- reduced handoffs through workflow consolidation
- structured tracking of progress and dependencies

---

## Example Flow

### AS-IS

Client request → Business team → Ops team → IT checks → Manual approvals → Delayed resolution

### TO-BE

Client request → Single intake point → Structured routing → Parallel processing → Clear status visibility

---

## Outcome (Expected)

- Faster turnaround time
- Reduced operational ambiguity
- Improved client experience consistency
- Lower dependency on manual coordination

---

## Learning

In regulated environments, the biggest gains come from simplifying coordination, not increasing system complexity.
